The Internship Experience

Hello, and welcome to my internship website! My name is Hunter John, and I want to study engineering with a focus on biomedical engineering. I am currently interning at the J. Craig Venter Institute (JCVI). My ultimate goal for this internship is to explore career options, and I believe this internship will help clarify my path in engineering or perhaps reveal new possibilities in technology and healthcare.

What I'm Doing

At my internship at the J. Craig Venter Institute (JCVI), I am involved in a project focused on the image classification of phytoplankton, specifically concentrating on diatoms and monitoring algal blooms. My role includes using advanced imaging and computational techniques to analyze images of these microorganisms. The data collected helps in identifying and cataloging different species of diatoms, which are a key component of phytoplankton and play a crucial role in aquatic ecosystems.

Additionally, my work assists in tracking and studying the development of algal blooms, which can have significant environmental impacts, such as affecting water quality and marine life. This involves applying mathematical models and machine learning algorithms to improve the detection and understanding of these phenomena, supporting broader environmental research and management efforts.
